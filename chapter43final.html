Chapter 42

Tara's wedding

Tara's Masters program was coming to an end soon. Many of Tara's classmates were going for further studies to earn a PhD. Some had moved to management studies. Some others had taken up well paying jobs. But Tara now was preparing for her wedding. She would think of her future plans after her marriage. All her career plans now would involve Arjun. It was a point of no return.

Tara and Arjun had decided on a simple court wedding with three witnesses. Tara was staying true to the oath she took many years ago of not paying a dowry which now also included not having a lavish wedding. Tara went to buy a mangalsutra (a gold chain married women wore) with Mala and Arjun.

"Please show us that mangalsutra" said Tara to the Goldsmith.

The Goldsmith looked at Tara incredulously and he brought out the mangalsutra out of the glass case hesitantly. Young people never went to buy mangalsutras on their own. If some one was getting married they would usually be accompanied by elders for purchasing gold.

"You look so young. He cannot believe that you are planning to get married. He is suspicious of the entire thing" said Mala laughing.

"I think I will take this one. It is nice and simple" said Tara choosing a mangalsutra.

"Wear it and see whether you like it" said Arjun.

"Oh no! One cannot wear it before one is married. It is a strict no, no" said Tara.

On the wedding day, Tara was sitting in the hostel mess and sipping tea. No one around her knew she was getting married. When she got back to her room she felt a catch at her stomach. She knew she was scared. She finished her packing. Arjun and Tara would leave for a short honeymoon as soon as they were married at the court. There was a knock at her door. She opened it to let Arjun in.

"I am scared, Arjun. This is a big step" said Tara.

"Do not worry. Everything will be fine" said Arjun.

Arjun carried Tara's bag outside and Tara saw a few friends waiting for them.

"She is scared" said Arjun laughing.

"Do not be afraid. We all are here to make sure you will be happy" said Sanjay who was a friend of Arjun. Somehow that statement was reassuring and Tara smiled.

"We should go, else we will be late. Hey, there is the bus. Let us catch it" said Sanjay.

The group ran to catch the bus and they managed to catch it. It so happened that they had to run to catch the train to town.

"It is so funny. No one I know ever ran to catch a bus and train on their wedding day" said Tara.

"Remember you wanted it this way" said Arjun.

"Yes, I like this part" said Tara.

When they reached town Arjun said

"We still have an hour to kill before the time allotted for our wedding in the court. Let us get something to eat."

The little group drank coconut water and ate pav bhaji from a street stall. The bill was fifty five rupees and Arjun paid the bill.

"Contrast fifty five rupees to a minimum of one lakh rupees people spend on weddings today. This sure is a smart way to get married" said Sanjay.

"Thanks, Arjun'' said Tara. "Many of my friends wanted to get married this way but when their time came, they could not because their husbands were not tough enough to withstand family pressures. I am glad, I am able to marry according to my principles."

When Tara and Arjun reached the judge's office, the clerk told them they had to fill a form. They filled the form in a hurry and went back to the judge, but they had to wait since someone else was getting married. But the wait was not long since civil marriage proceedings lasted only five minutes. When it was Arjun and Tara's turn they had to sign a document and read a simple one line oath each. The three witnesses signed the same document and the judge congratulated them and it was over.

Tara was in a daze when she stepped out of the office and Arjun put the mangalsutra around Tara's neck. She was now married. There was a little celebration and people were taking pictures of her and talking. But Tara heard nothing.

Soon it was time to leave for the honeymoon. Tara and Arjun left the little crowd of friends behind to catch a boat to Alibaug.

"Wait here. I will get the tickets for the boat" said Arjun and left the bags with Tara.

Tara could not believe she was married. She held the mangalsutra around her neck in her hand for sometime.

"Am I married? Really?" she asked herself.

She looked around and it was a strange place. The crowd around her was strange. She felt the catch in her stomach again. What was she doing here? She wanted to go back home when she saw Arjun's smiling face return. He had the tickets in his hands. He put it in his wallet, picked up all the bags, and said

"Let us go, Tara. My wife."

Tara smiled and gave him her hand. It was a new life and a new beginning.

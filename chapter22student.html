Chapter 22

Tara's student

Bhandiwallas were people who would exchange steel vessels for old clothes. Tara's mother had summoned a bhandiwalla to get rid of all the unused and discarded clothes in the house.

"Give me that big pot" said Tara's mother to the bhandiwalla.

"These old clothes are not enough for that pot" said the bhandiwalla. "Do you have any more old saris?"

"I do not have any more saris to give away. But I have a few old aluminum vessels if you like."

"Show me the vessels."

Tara's mother brought out some old broken aluminum pots. The Bhandiwalla agreed to give Tara's mother the steel pot she wanted in exchange of the clothes and aluminum pots. Tara's mother held the pot in her hand and she looked happy with it.

"Mother, I do not understand why you like vessels and pots so much" said Tara.

"When you get married I have to give you pots and pans" said Tara's mother. "I am collecting for it."

"Why do you always think of my marriage? Why are you in such a hurry to get rid of me?"

"That is a mother's job. To worry about her daughter's marriage."

"Do I get this pot as dowry?" asked Tara with a grin.

"You can have it if you want."

"In general I am against dowry. But I think I might accept this pot. Let me consider it."

Tara then walked around with the pot in her hand saying

"My dowry. My dowry. My dowry."

"Stop it Tara" said her mother laughing.

Just then Seema Akka came to the door with another lady and said

"This lady wants someone to teach her daughter math and science. Do you think Tara can teach?"

"Ask Tara" said Tara's mother. "There she is playing with a pot."

"Seema Akka. Look at my dowry" said Tara showing her the pot.

"Chee! How can you talk about your dowry so openly? You should be more shy about such things. When someone mentions marriage a girl should bend down her head and say issh. The girls these days are just too much" said Seema Akka scandalized.

"Okay. Issh if you like. But look at my dowry pot."

Tara laughed and handed the pot to Seema Akka to take a closer look.

"You want me to teach math and science to your daughter?" Tara asked the lady who came with Seema Akka. "I have never taught before. It might be fun. Is your daughter good at studies?"

"She is very hard working but she is weak in math. How much fees will you take?"

Tara thought for a minute but she had no idea what to expect and so she asked

"What are you paying right now?"

"Two hundred and fifty rupees. But I will pay you three hundred since you have no other student. Is that fine?"

"Sounds fine" said Tara.

"Okay. So she will come for lessons from tomorrow. Thank you."

When the lady left Tara said

"I will make three hundred rupees per month from now on. I am going to be rich. Meet rich Tara from now on."

"I would have thought that lady would refuse to trust you with her daughter's education after seeing you walk around with a pot" said Tara's mother laughing.

"This is serious responsibility" said Tara worried. "I hope I can teach well. I am scared."

Tara's mother looked at Tara and realized this was time for motherly encouragement and said

"Tara, dear child. you will do just fine."

So the next day Tara's student turned up at five in the evening. When Tara went to the living room to meet her student she saw this little girl sitting on the chair with her arms folded. Her books were on the table. The books were all covered neatly with brown paper and had pretty labels on them.

"What is your name?" asked Tara.

"Smita" said the little girl.

Tara wondered about whether she should be friendly or act strict and grim. Tara was at a loss on how to behave. The little girl clearly was in awe of her. Tara decided to stick to math.

"So what did your teacher do in class today?" Tara asked.

Smita showed her a chapter from her textbook. Tara assigned some problems and asked her to do it and Smita did all of them correctly.

"You know how to do all these problems" said Tara. "I do not know what to teach you. How come they say you are weak in math? Let us do this. You try out the problems in the book and if you are stuck ask me."

Smita could do almost all the problems herself and the few questions she had were really easy to answer. Tara once in a while asked questions from Smita's science notebook and Smita knew all answers. Within a few weeks Smita started doing very well in school to Tara's pleasant surprise because as far as Tara could tell Smita did everything on her own. Tara hardly ever taught her anything. One day Tara asked her

"Smita. Tell me. You are a good student. How come you did not do this well before? You took tuition from someone else and how did that person teach you?"

"That lady would give me ten problems to do ten times each and turn in every week and if I did not do it she would hit me" said Smita.

"She would hit you? That is wrong. Also it is silly to do the same problem ten times."

"Yes" said Smita.

"Did it hurt?" asked Tara.

"A little. She would hit us on the palm with a ruler."

"She has a lot of students though. I still cannot believe that she could hit you. Did you tell your mother about it?"

"No. Mothers usually aggravate your problems instead of helping out."

"That might be true" said Tara laughing.

"But I am glad you are my teacher now."

"I am glad you are my student too. But I think you are getting the good grades on your own. I was trying to find the reason why you could not get good grades on your own before."

"I think I like it that you think I am good in math. That way I want to be good in math. I like it when you are amazed that I can solve all the problems. It gives me confidence and I continue to do better."

"I am sure glad this works for you. You really are a good student. Now let us be serious and do more math."

Tara and Smita put on a grim face and they both laughed the next minute.

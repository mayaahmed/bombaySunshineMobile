Chapter 30

Hostel life

Finally the day arrived when Tara had to move to the hostel. Tara's brother dropped her to the hostel. All the excitement of hostel life vanished when she saw her brother to the gate. She became homesick and wanted to go back with Babu. It was raining heavily and Tara suddenly wanted to cry. But she started to unpack instead. Soon there was a knock at her door. The seniors were going from room to room to meet the new girls.

"I am Tara. I am a first year masters student" Tara introduced herself.

"We could do with a Tara" said one senior. "We do not have a Tara yet but we have three Ashas. It is good you are not Asha. We cannot deal with another Asha."

"Are you Asha?" asked Tara unable to resist a wise crack.

"Do not ask questions. Speak only when spoken to" said the senior.

"I thought you were talking to me. Is there someone behind me?" asked Tara looking behind and this made some seniors laugh.

"You are alright Tara. We will now introduce ourselves. I am Jyoti, this is Lata, this is Rajini, and this is Dina."

"Are you arranging your room?" asked Lata. "Keep up the good work. By the way, you will need curtains. The boys out here can be quite crazy. They ogle at you using binoculars. You see the ratio of boys to girls in this campus is quite bad."

"I will keep that in mind" said Tara. "Thank you."

"Count on us if you need anything Tara" said Rajini. "We are going to like having you around."

"Go and get you identity card from the administration building" said Jyoti. "That is the first thing you should get. You will need an identity card all the time. It is your ticket to the library, to the movie shows, to the swimming pool, you name it, the identity card is everything. If you lose your identity card it is like losing your identity."

Tara had to stand in a line to get the identity card. There were many people waiting to get their identity cards.

"Are you new to the campus?" asked a boy standing behind her.

"Yes. Are you new too?" asked Tara.

"No, I just lost my identity card" said the boy.

When Tara got her identity card she said to the boy "I do not want to lose my identity. I am going to be real careful about my identity card."

And to Tara's surprise the boy started singing

"Phir miloge kabhi, is baat ka vaada karlo,
Apni ek aur mulaqat ka vaada karlo
(Promise you will meet me again,
Promise me one more meeting)."

The seniors were right. There were very few girls in the campus. It was a big deal for a boy to have a conversation with a girl. No matter how trivial the conversation was. Tara just shrugged and went back to her room. When she had finished arranging her room it was time for dinner but she did not feel like getting out of the room. She really was homesick.

Finally, Tara decided to go to the dining hall. There were many rows of tables in the hall. A lot of girls were having dinner and it was quite noisy. For chapatis (wheat bread) every one had to go in to the kitchen. Tara saw Mala standing in a line waiting for chapatis.

"I did not know you are already here" said Tara. "It is good to see a familiar face. Boy! Am I homesick?"

"I am glad to see you too" said Mala.

The cooks surrounded a very large pan and they were making hot chapatis and when it was ready they would put it on the girl's plates. When Tara's turn came the main cook asked

"How many chapatis do you want?"

Tara said "Two."

"Here comes two chapatis for you" said the cook. "So you are a new student. Welcome to the hostel. What is your name?"

"Tara."

"Are you are from the south?" asked the cook.

"Yes."

"I could tell. I can always tell our girls from the south. Good. Okay Tara, hope you like the food we cook."

"I am sure I will."

The cook took an immediate liking to Tara mainly because she was from the south. Tara took sabjis which were kept on a counter in the dining hall in really huge vessels. Rice and dal (lentils) were also kept in huge vessels. The curd however was kept in little bowls on a separate table with a note

"Please take one bowl each only."

"This hostel seems alright" said Mala.

"Let us go for a walk after dinner" said Lata. "That is the best thing about this campus. It is safe to go out even at midnight."

"That will be nice" said Tara.

"If you like music, you can get the key to the music cupboard from me" continued Lata. "I am the music secretary. And for dinner you can listen to the disc Call of the valley, a favorite of mine."

Saying this Lata went and opened a glass cupboard in the end of the dining hall and she played the music disc.

"It is beautiful music" said Tara. "The flute sounds make me see mirages of a mountain valley in my mind."

"That is why I like it so much" said Lata.

Later the girls went for a long walk and Lata showed them all the important buildings of the campus.

"It is so quiet" said Tara. "It is difficult to believe that this campus is part of Bombay."

Suddenly a boy came up to them and asked them their names.

"Why do you want to know it?" asked Lata angrily.

"I am being ragged" He whispered. "I am a freshie. Sorry about this."

Sure enough there were a bunch of seniors standing a little away. So Tara and her friends gave him some fake names and they walked ahead. But soon they found him running behind them and he asked

"Is there any wish you people have? Any command?"

Then Lata came up with an idea and she shouted aloud "We do not want you to be ragged anymore."

"One wish granted. He will not be ragged for today at least" said a grinning senior.

There was not much ragging in Tara's hostel, fortunately. The first week was all about welcome functions.

"We have attended too many welcome functions" said Tara. "Campus welcome function, Department welcome function, Freshies welcome function, so on and so forth. All these functions hardly make me welcome though. I miss home like hell."

"Me too" said Mala. "Moreover we have to put up shows for these functions. It sure is weird. If they are welcoming us they should act out a play, sing a song, etc. They expect us to entertain them. Some welcome!"

"What should we do for the hostel welcome function on Thursday?" asked Meena.

"Let us do a Kohli (Fisherwoman) dance" said Mala. "That is the easiest dance to learn."

Came Thursday and so did the welcome party. Tara and her friends did a Kohli dance which they managed to learn in an hour and the seniors were quite pleased with the effort. The Thursday dinner was called Dry dinner which was a strange combination of pulao, potato vada, chutney, fruit custard, and gulab jamun.

"They must be giving us this good dinner because they want us to come back next week" said Meena and everyone laughed. On Friday they were more than willing to go home. Tara was really happy to be home with her mother.

"Ma. I missed you so much" said Tara.

"I missed you a lot too Tara" said Tara's mother. "I did not feel like doing anything this whole week. My child, you are my source of joy."

"Nothing like a little distance to make the heart fonder" said Tara. "All this time you said mean things about me."

The ball was in Tara's court and she was feeling rather important. Her mother did not make any sarcastic remarks because she really had missed Tara quite a bit.

That weekend was rather a relaxing weekend since everyone gave Tara a lot of importance and told her how much they missed her. But time passed and Tara could see that nature abhorred vacuum and her space in the house was slowly disappearing. Her shelves and table were all gone.

"Soon you people will forget that I even lived here" sulked Tara.

"Do not say such hurting things" said Tara's father. "What is the point in keeping shelves empty? When you come back from the hostel we will empty any cupboard you like."

"Yes, I know. But I just said that to remind you that I exist if you have chosen to forget about me."

Back at the hostel too, the dry dinners were not as interesting as it was at first.

"I am sick of the same pulao, potato vada, fruit custard, and gulab jamun" said Tara. "It even tastes the same every Thursday. I do not know why I particularly hate dry dinners."

"I hate it too" said Mala. "I would not feed this to my worst enemy. That gives me an idea. We should feed dry dinner to our classmate Buntu. That will serve him right for being so obnoxious."

This idea created a lot of mirth for Tara and Mala.

"He will say good good after every bite he takes in that sick voice" said Mala.

Tara imitated Buntu saying good good.

"Buntu will be so happy he is invited to dinner" said Mala. "Little will he know that he is going to be fed dry dinner. He will choke after the first mouth full."

"Yes, After he says good good good he will choke" said Tara.

Mala and Tara laughed and laughed at the thought of inviting Buntu to dry dinner.

Later that night Tara wrote in her diary

"For some reason I am not able to open my soul to this new crowd. My conversations are frivolous. There is not enough time to make lasting friendships. The whole exercise of making friends seem somehow futile. Losing my good friends again and again over time is making it hard for me to make real connections. I might be one of the most popular girls in the hostel and yet what is the measure of popularity? It is reduced to the number of people you smile at and exchange a meaningless greeting. There are people around me who envy me even for that. I think I may get out of here without having made a real friend. Why did I change so suddenly? Is it me or is it the crowd? I will never know."

But as time went by Tara met many interesting people who would leave a lasting impression on her in spite of her initial resistance to connect with people.

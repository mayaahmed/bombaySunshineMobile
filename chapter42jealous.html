Chapter 42

Mine and only mine

It became a routine for Arjun to wait for Tara outside her department and walk back with her towards the hostel. Invariably he would convince her to go to a new place with him. Today they had walked the whole length of the Pod lake. Arjun and Tara was sitting besides the lake watching the sunset.

"It is so quiet and peaceful here" said Tara. "The reflection of the sunset on the lake is mind blowing. I think I am going to become a hermit. You know, live in a little hut in a forest. It will be so much fun."

"We will be able to manage that" said Arjun.

Tara was a little surprised. Why was Arjun considering himself part of Tara's plans of being a hermit? Tara decided to let go of that question and went back to watching the sunset.

"Hi Tara" called out Meena from far.

"Hi" said Tara back but Meena did not linger. She just left the place with a meaningful grin. This was getting to be embarrassing for Tara. What would Arjun think? He was just a friend.

"Let us get some dinner" said Arjun.

All the restaurants were situated outside the gates of the campus and Tara and Arjun walked towards the gates.

"This is a good place. The food is good here" said Arjun stopping by a real small shanty restaurant.

"Really. Does anyone eat here?" asked Tara in surprise.

"Yes, it is a great place" said Arjun and they entered the small dingy place.

"You know I feel very brave entering this place. None of my girlfriends would ever think of eating in a place like this."

"Get us a plate of daal (lentils) fry, tomato fry, and some rotis" ordered Arjun.

"You know, none of my girl friends would order dishes like that at a restaurant" said Tara.

"What do girls eat?" asked Arjun.

"Chana masala, paneer kofta, or something fancy like that. Ordering daal fry would never occur to any of us. This is so macho."

"We eat here all the time."

Arjun was a little amused by Tara's comments. Tara looked around and found that she was the only female in the place and a few people were actually watching her. But she was not afraid. Somehow She knew she was safe wherever she was if she was with Arjun. She also enjoyed the meal Arjun had ordered for them.

"Tomorrow we can go to Koldi. You will really like that place" said Arjun.

Tara thought of Meena's meaningful grin and she realized that she was spending way too much time with Arjun. This was not right. So she said

"Arjun, may be I am getting addicted to you. Let us keep some distance."

To Tara's surprise Arjun did not like that statement at all. He was angry and looked hurt. After dinner, instead of walking back to the hostels he walked towards the woods.

"It is too late to be romping in the forests" said Tara. "There are snakes and also a leopard. Please let us go back to the hostel."

Tara was completely at a loss about Arjun's anger.

"I do not care" said Arjun.

He walked among the tall grass deeper and deeper into the woods and Tara followed. When they reached the boat house Arjun stopped and he sat down on the cement floor and Tara sat beside him.

"I am sorry" said Tara. "I do not know what I said to make you angry."

"You know Tara, I intend to marry you" said Arjun.

Tara was taken aback. It was not like that she was surprised for she knew that Arjun was special. It was only a week since she had met him and she had spend more time with him than any other man she had known. Every day they had romped the woods or gone for walks along lake sides. It had been a week long trip of discovering nature for Tara. She had enjoyed Arjun's company and yet she had not wanted marriage. It was way too soon.

"Arjun, I do not want to marry" said Tara. "It will interfere with my plans. I am sure I will be a lousy wife. I want to travel to remote places and marriage will not work for me. Why can't we be just good friends?"

"Friends I have plenty. You, I want to marry" said Arjun.

"I do not believe in marriage. I have seen couples fight so much. Sometimes I think it is better to have an enemy than a spouse. I don't want to marry, Arjun."

"That is not true, Tara. There are really happy couples. A lot of my friends are really happily married. I am sure we will be happy together. You will be free to do what you want. You can travel, explore, and study even after we are married."

"No, not marriage. I have not seen a single ideal couple in my life yet."

"Okay, think about it. I want to marry you and I am sure we will be happy. Let us go back. It is getting late. There is a full moon up there tonight luckily. We can avoid stepping on snakes."

Arjun knew that his proposal had gone well. He had made his point and now it was just a matter of time. On the way he met some friends and he introduced Tara to everyone. After they left he said

"Now I can introduce you to people. I did not want any competition before I proposed to you."

Tara had to laugh. They did not talk about marriage again when they met the next day. Arjun gave Tara a book and said

"Here is a book for you. It is a children's book called the Little prince. Let us say the book's philosophy is my philosophy too. I hope you like the book."

"What is it about?" asked Tara.

"It is about a prince and his love for a rose. He has a garden and I have one too. Would you like to see it?"

"Sure" said Tara.

Arjun took her to the backyard of his hostel and said "Welcome to my garden, Tara."

"Where is your garden?" asked Tara a little surprised.

"You are standing in it. The garden is kind of new and the plants are small" said Arjun.

Tara noticed that the land she was standing on was cleared of weeds and there were some small plants growing around.

"This is a Papaya tree. This is a Hibiscus plant" said Arjun pointing them to Tara.

"You need to put up a sign board. It is hard to tell it is a garden" said Tara laughing.

"Every man should have a garden. It is good for the soul. Voltaire says that in Candida too."

"I guess it must be fun to have a garden."

Tara was a little intrigued by Arjun and his ways. He was rich and intelligent and yet so simple. So different from most boys she had known before. He was also easy to get along with.

A couple of days later Tara felt like looking pretty. She looked through her wardrobe and she picked out a yellow sari.

"I have a boyfriend now. I can afford to look pretty" said Tara to Mala.

Tara knew she looked good but she had not expected the effect it would have on Arjun. He was really thrilled.

"You look incredible, Tara. I have never seen anyone look so good in a sari before" said Arjun.

"I just felt like dressing up a little today" said Tara with a grin.

"You look incredible" said Arjun again.

The two of them walked towards a pond Tara called their pond. They had spend every afternoon besides that pond for a week now.

"I wonder why people do not take time off to sit besides a pond like this" said Tara. "Look at the beauty nature has to offer us. The butterflies, the flowers, the songs of the birds. Yet, man has no time. All other living beings spend time appreciating nature and her seasonal gifts except people."

A minute later Arjun had taken Tara's hand in his hand and tied a little rope he had made of grass around her finger and he said

"Tara, we are engaged."

It was too nice a moment to say anything.

"We are engaged?" asked Tara softly after sometime.

"Yes, I will get you another ring soon. But this rope ring will do for now" said Arjun.

"Engagements are a big thing in my family" said Tara. "Lots of people come over and there is usually a big party. This is surely a quaint way of getting engaged."

"We must meet each others family soon" said Arjun.

"I need to break this news to my mother. It is going to be tough since we are not from the same religion. My father might ship me off to a village or something. I am worried" said Tara.

"They will never be able to do that. I am a tough guy" said Arjun.

The meeting with the families were not smooth but Tara and Arjun had handled it well. As days went by they found it harder and harder to stay away from each other.

Tara's friends excused her for staying away from many major events. But for an important function in her department she decided to stay and help. She was busy the entire day.

"Tara, let us go for a walk" said Arjun.

"No, Arjun" said Tara. "I cannot leave now. What will my friends think? I need to help out. It is my duty. I will be busy all day."

"I will be back in one hour" said Arjun and he was back after half hour.

"Tara, you may as well leave. Arjun is not going to let you do any work" said Mala laughing.

"Arjun, you are impossible. Don't you see I have work to do? What will my friends think?" asked Tara.

"I do not care. I cannot stay away from you for so long" said Arjun.

So Tara excused herself from the jobs assigned to her and left with Arjun. After all nothing or no one mattered more to her than Arjun anymore.

Time and again Tara's life before Arjun resurfaced and started making demands on her time. It was a growing conflict she could not ignore.

"Tara, please participate in the Performing Arts festival with us. Please, please" said Monika who was a junior.

"I cannot" said Tara. "I do not have the time. Anyways it is for juniors. We did our parts last year. Now you people do yours."

"But we need enthusiastic people like you" said Monika.

"No. I do not have time" said Tara escaping from the little crowd of juniors around her.

"They want me to participate in the Performing Arts festival. I said no" explained Tara to Arjun.

"Good. All that is a waste of time" said Arjun.

"Being on Stage is an interesting feeling. I just did not want to get involved this year, that is all. I would rather spend the time with you" said Tara smiling.

But a few days later the juniors had surrounded Tara again.

"Tara, do not act in the play. But we want you to be part of the advertising campaign. This will take no time. You just have to dress up as an angel and stand on top of a high stage. You can do this much for the hostel" said Monika.

"Okay" said Tara hesitantly.

"It is tonight. It will only take an hour of your time. Thanks a lot" said Monika really happy.

That night Tara dressed up as an angel. She wore a flowing white robe and let her hair loose. Arjun and Tara waited outside the auditorium for a play performed by a competing group to get over.

"How do I look?" asked Tara.

"Good. You always look good" said Arjun.

The students had constructed a high stage for her and had surrounded it with bright lights.

"When I am up there, I will wave to you. It is so high, I am scared if I fall I will die" said Tara.

Then it was time to be on stage. A group of people came to fetch Tara.

"I will wave to you from above. Do not move from this spot" said Tara calling out to Arjun.

Her voice was drowning in the shouts of the directors of the show. And soon she found herself on top of the stage looking down and a huge crowd had gathered below. Her stunt was a huge success. But where was Arjun? He was nowhere to be seen. For a long time, Tara searched the crowd for Arjun and she did not see him. Then she waited for the show to end. She did not care anymore.

As soon as the show was over, Tara went to Arjun's hostel. It was around ten in the night but she did not care. She went and knocked at his door and when Arjun opened the door she said

"It was a hit. Why did you not wait for me? Did you see it was a hit?"

"Yes" said Arjun. "But when the crowd came to fetch you, I thought I had lost you forever. I felt like you were gone forever."

"I will never leave you, Arjun" said Tara giving him a hug.

After sometime Tara said "Gee, It is really late. I should not have come here, now. People will gossip."

"Wait for half an hour. This is the late canteen hour, the corridors will be full of people. After that it should be safe to go" said Arjun.

So after half hour, Arjun went and checked the corridors and then the two of them slowly tiptoed out of the hostel. The watchman was fast asleep at his chair.

"Thank god for sleeping watchmen" said Tara laughing.

A few days later the juniors were after Tara again.

"Tara, will you be part of a crowd in our play today? We really are short of people" said Monika.

"No. No. I cannot. Arjun is really possessive about me. I cannot take part in the play" said Tara.

"Well, you just have to be part of a crowd. There is nothing to it. It is a heaven scene and we need a crowd. Please, Tara. We really need people" said Monika.

"Okay, let me ask Arjun" said Tara.

When Tara called up Arjun and asked him, he said that was fine. He said he will come to see the play too.

So Tara dressed up as a goddess and she went along to be a part of the crowd. Some people picked the girls from the hostel and Tara was one of the last to reach backstage.

"Come, come. You are in the first scene. It is getting late" shouted the director. When Tara entered the stage she was given the center seat and she was surrounded by angels. No way was she part of a crowd. She could not tell whether she was a queen or a chief goddess for she had no idea what the play was about.

She quite enjoyed the play when it started. It was about gods who were having some dispute and it was funny. And when the scene ended a couple of gods were punished to go to earth and Tara's role in the play was over. She went down to search for Arjun.

When she found Arjun, he did not look too happy.

"That was not part of being a crowd. You were center stage" said Arjun.

"I did not know that they were going to ask me to sit in the center. Monika said I was going to be part of a crowd. Anyways, someone had to sit in the center" said Tara and tickled Arjun till he smiled.

After sometime Tara said

"Boy, it is tough to ride a mobike in a costume. You tend to feel you will fall off."

"What? You came here on someone's mobike?" asked Arjun really shocked.

"Yes. I could not possibly walk on the street in a costume like this" said Tara.

That was it. Arjun was very angry.

"All girls are like this. I want to have nothing to do with you" said Arjun and he walked off in a huff.

Tara did not know what to do. Then she saw the little tea shop where students would gather to drink tea at all the odd hours of the day or night. The shop sold nothing but tea, a few biscuits, and chocolates. She wanted to buy something for Arjun and she went and bought all the chocolates in the jar in the shop for him.

Arjun was quite happy with the gift and more than willing to forgive and forget. He even shared some of the chocolates with his friends.

"No girl has ever bought me chocolates before" said Arjun with a shy smile.

Chapter 26

Tara's summer job

Tara had applied for a summer job as a student trainee at a bank. She was very happy when she got selected for the job. On the first day of her summer job she tied her hair up in a bun to look more grown up. Her mother looked at her with pride in her eyes.

"I am going to work for the first time in my life for an actual salary" said Tara. "I have thousands of plans with the money I make."

"Tara, it is only a stipend. How can you make thousands of plan with so little?" asked her mother.

"There is no reason why there should be a limit to making plans" said Tara. "Of course executing those plans may not be possible. Let us not curtail dreams. Dreams are the only things that are free in this world and let them stay so."

Tara's mother beamed with pride as Tara set out of the house. When Tara reached the bank, she went to the counter and the clerk looked up at her and asked

"Do you want to make a deposit?"

"No" said Tara. "I am a student trainee. I have come here to work."

"Oh, come on in then" said the clerk with a smile. He then shouted

"Hey, Madam. There is a student trainee here for you."

The lady who the clerk called madam was a manager. She was scolding some employees. When Tara saw this she was a little frightened of the manager.

"That woman is a terror" said the clerk looking at Tara's frightened face. "But do not worry. You will not have to work with her for long. You just meet her."

Soon Tara was given a desk of her own and there were a few files in front of her. The pages inside the files contained many columns of numbers and it was Tara's job to calculate the totals of each column.

"Is there a calculator around?" asked Tara.

"Are you in college?" asked the manager.

"Yes."

"And you want a calculator to add? See these people around you? They have not even passed their twelfth standard and they can add without a calculator. Why can't you?"

"Well, I thought it just might be easier."

"If you cannot add easily then you might as well give up and go home."

Tara got angry and she wanted to walk out of the door. But just in time the clerk interfered and said

"You will not need a calculator. The additions are easy."

"Okay" said Tara still a little angry with the woman.

After some time a little boy came and kept half a cup of tea in front of Tara and said

"Cutting (a half cup of tea was called cutting)."

Tara looked up in surprise. The boy said

"All bank employees get free cuttings twice a day. I am the chaiwallah (Tea seller)."

Tara took the cup of tea and drank it. It made her feel better. She was tired after turning so many pages, totaling, and checking again and again for errors. She had the reputation of her education at stake. She was afraid that the lady would find faults with her calculations. Whenever Tara looked up at the lady, the lady made an expression of disdain. That was her style. She would make angry faces at everyone and poor Tara was at a loss how to behave.

Well that was Tara's first day at work and when she got home she was so tired that she went to sleep without eating her supper.

"Hey, it is a bank job" exclaimed Tara's mother. "How come you are so tired child?"

"Bending down and turning pages and totaling is tiring if one has to do that for eight hours in a row" said Tara.

"Don't go if you don't want to" said Tara's mother.

"If others can do it, I can do it too" said Tara.

But soon Tara found out why others were not tired at the end of the day. They took long tea breaks every hour. Some people would act as if they were working but would actually be sleeping behind their files. But it seemed like they had sensors that could tell where the boss was. As soon as a supervisor surfaced a sleeping clerk would wake up immediately and act as though he was immersed in work. All this would tickle Tara's humor and she would think about some people and laugh all the way home. There were a couple of other student trainees and Tara became good friends with them.

"That manager really frowns at me" said Tara. "I do not know what I ever did to her."

"That is her style. She frowns at me too" said another student trainee.

Other student trainees had the same problem with the manager's frowns.

"Let us teach her a lesson" said Tara. "Let us give her a taste of her own medicine. I am going to frown at her too the next time she looks at me. You people do the same."

This created a lot of mirth among the student trainees. When they got back to the office after their lunch they set the plan in to action. Now all the other trainees managed to frown at the manager quite well but the manager did not find anything amiss. The girls giggled to themselves. Tara also wanted to do her part of frowning at the manager but every time the lady looked at her Tara started giggling. This was irritating to the manager and she kept looking at Tara which made Tara laugh more.

Finally Tara decided to think of some real sad incident in her life and soon she managed to stop the giggling fit. Tara then frowned at the manager in a big way. That was it. Tara burst out laughing and went to the inside lounge and laughed and laughed. Her friends followed and they all laughed. After sometime the manager surfaced. Tara whispered to her friends "She is going to yell and scream now."

But to everyone's surprise the manager waved and said hello with the brightest smile ever.

"Talk about surprising behaviors" exclaimed Tara.

Soon Tara had her paycheck and she decided to take her cousin Veena for a treat.

"Let us go to a five star hotel" said Tara. "After that we will do some shopping. I need to buy a skirt."

"Let us go to Taj" said Veena. "We could go and get a couple of soft drinks and enjoy the ambiance."

Veena went to an up town college. Her friends were mostly fashion models and Veena knew all about the good life compared to Tara.

"Look, I have a salary" said Tara. "We really can afford to eat something."

So they ordered a plate of little pizzas, a few samosas, a couple of soft drinks, and in the end even got a pastry each. After all how much can it cost? Well it took care of all of Tara's salary and Veena offered to pay part of the bill.

"No. No. This is my treat" said Tara and watched her salary disappear.

"I might as well forget my shopping. But the ambiance was fun" said Tara.

But on their return trip from town, Veena disappeared for a few minutes to return with a package. When Tara opened the package she found a beautiful skirt. She exclaimed

"This was exactly what I wanted. That took care of my shopping. Thank you Veena."

"All is well that ends well" said Veena.

The next month Tara was very careful with her money. She bought a ceiling fan for her house.

"Sometimes the most mundane things become so important" said Tara. "I am so proud that I bought that fan with my pay check. I have never cared about a fan in my life before. There are so many other fans in the world but this fan is so special because I bought it."

"Fruits of hard work are sweet" said Tara's mother.

Soon Tara's summer job was ending. Tara had learned a lot at her job. She also enjoyed her work. Tara's latest assignment was to work at the customer service desk. It was her job to give out receipts and put seals on checks the bank got each day. Tara particularly enjoyed stamping checks. When she was done with putting a stamp on all the checks for the day she would practice putting seals on a blank paper. She wanted her stamps to look perfect all the time.

"The right pressure, the right amount of ink and lo, we get a perfect seal" said Tara to a neighboring clerk.

"I have never seen anyone so happy working with a stamp" said the clerk laughing.

"Putting seals on something makes you feel important. For some reason I like it better than signatures" said Tara.

"Your training is getting over soon. Will you come back to work for us after your studies?" asked the clerk.

"I do not know" said Tara.

"Are you going to give us a farewell party?" asked the clerk. Tara gulped remembering how her salary had vanished at the five star hotel treat she gave her cousin.

"Do not worry" said the clerk. We know your stipend is not big. Just buy us cuttings."

"That we sure can do" said Tara happily. She quickly got together with other student trainees and made arrangements for tea and some snacks for the office people for the next day.

"Our bank job gets over tomorrow" said Tara to the student trainees. "It is at such times that I understand the concept of illusion or Maya. I came to this place every day for three months. I was with these people for three months. May be after tomorrow I will not see them ever again. Was this three months real? Did it matter whether it was real or not?"

"Poet!" said one student trainee.

"Philosopher" said another.

The next day the student trainees treated the office staff with tea and snacks. The office staff profusely thanked the students. When everyone started to go back to their desks to work, the manager said

"Wait, wait, wait. We have something for these hard working students."

She gave a little speech about how good it had been to have the student trainees around and she gave each of them a pen set.

When Tara showed her mother the pen set she said

"Keep it carefully. That is a real nice pen set you got."

That night Tara wrote in her diary

"Everything seems like an illusion. Just like how one wakes up from a dream and there are a few traces left, this pen set and that circulating fan are traces of my wonderful dream of a summer job at a bank."

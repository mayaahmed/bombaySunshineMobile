Chapter 36

Visiting an orphanage

It was the Onam week. The hostel was organizing a grand lunch for Onam. The Malayali girls were planning to make rangoli with flowers and perform Kaikottikali. Kaikottikali was a dance where people went around in circles clapping their hands and wearing a mundu set which was the traditional dress of Kerala. But Tara went home for Onam. She helped her mother prepare the grand Onam meal. Tara's friends Seeta, Sarita, and Seema came over for the Onam lunch.

"We are going to eat anj curry, inj narangya" said Tara.

"What is that?" asked Seema.

"Anj curry means five curries, inj means ginger, and narangya means lemon" said Tara. "This is the minimum requirements for a Onam meal. It has to be all vegetarian."

"I like Onam" said Sarita. "The fact that all Malayalees celebrate Onam irrespective of religion is an example for the rest of the country. Keralites are very secular that way."

"The story goes that a king visits every year and he would be sad if he saw the people of Kerala not happy" said Seeta. "So during Onam even the poor eat well. The rich make sure they do."

"It is basically a harvest festival" said Seema.

"That is right" said Sarita. "It is a harvest festival. People all over the world have harvest festivals. Harvests are time to celebrate."

"It does not make sense to us. We know nothing about harvests" said Seema.

"But we know how to celebrate what we know nothing of" said Tara laughing.

Tara's mother placed mats on the floor and served lunch on banana leaves.

"No dining tables today. Must sit on the floor with legs folded" said Tara.

"I love eating out of banana leaves" said Sarita.

"When you are done you fold your leaf so that they wont serve you further" said Sarita.

When Tara saw all the food served on her leaf, she told her mother

"This is way beyond anj curry inj narangya. You cooked ten curries at least."

"Onam only comes once a year" Tara's mother said smiling.

After Lunch Sarita was planning to visit an orphanage. Time and again Tara had helped Sarita with flood reliefs or collecting money for charity. But Tara had never been to an orphanage before.

"Can we come along?" asked Tara.

"If you want to" said Sarita.

Tara, Seeta, and Seema decided to follow Sarita to the orphanage that day. The orphanage was a tiny apartment with only a couple of rooms. Most children were sleeping in cradles and a few children were on the floor. Tara noticed that a child was crying out loud. When Tara asked why the child was crying a maid said that the child was hungry. The maid said that she was warming milk to give it to the child.

Tara went near the cradle and peeped at the child. The child was a beautiful girl with really long lashes. Seeing a human face the child smiled. Tara lifted the child up and the child continued to smile. The child's smile somehow pained Tara. It was a smile of utter loneliness and abandonment. It was a smile which said I do not want to bother you the least, please like me. It was somehow misplaced in a little infant. Tara played with the child for sometime and Tara did not feel like keeping the child down all through her visit. Tara looked around the little orphanage and in one room she saw some women and children.

"These mothers are here only for a month" explained Sarita. "They will give up their children to the orphanage soon. We let them stay here for sometime since it is good for children to be with their birth mothers as long as possible."

"I wonder why they are giving up their children" said Tara.

"Girl child. If you notice the majority of the children here are girls. Unwed mothers are another reason" explained Sarita.

Tara watched the women who were giving up their children. She could not tell that they had any compulsions or sorrows. Most women tried to make themselves useful as much as possible. They helped with the tailoring and taking care of other children. They laughed at jokes but they would never mention why they were giving up a child.

It was time for Tara to go back and the little child that Tara was carrying around had in that short time formed a strong bond with Tara. Tara put the child back in the cradle with great difficulty. When Tara got out of the orphanage she sat down on a bench a little overwhelmed.

"Depressing" said Tara. "This visit is really depressing. I feel like I just left a room filled with an atmosphere of choking misery."

"It is tough on people. Orphanages are not picnic spots" said Sarita calmly.

"I am sorry Sarita" said Tara. "I think I am kind but I am not strong. My kindness is of no purpose. But if you are strong and not kind you might be of greater value to society than being a barrel full of emotions like me. If you are kind and strong then it is the best way to be. I fail in emotional strength and I feel like a wimp."

"Each person is made for a different purpose" said Sarita. "This is one of the good orphanages where the children are treated well. Believe me it can get worse than this."

"Do you get depressed?" asked Tara.

"Sometimes I do. But some of us have to be strong. Don't we?" asked Sarita smiling.

"Yes" said Tara quietly.

"Now I have to go. I have to go to a meeting" said Sarita.

Tara and her friends walked back home each of them lost in their own thoughts talking very little to each other. The visit to the orphanage had affected them in a way more than they ever thought it would.

When Tara reached home she gave her mother a hug and said

"Thank you, ma."

Her mother was a little surprised for she had no idea that Tara had visited an orphanage.

"Oh, did you really like the Onam lunch then?" asked Tara's mother.

"Yes, after the hostel food this is manna from heaven" said Tara laughing.
